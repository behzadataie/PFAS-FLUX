# Hydrogeology Journal technical note positioning

This repository supports a Hydrogeology Journal technical note. The manuscript should be framed as a hydrogeologic workflow, not as a regulatory guidance note.

Recommended framing:

- Mass flux and mass discharge are established concepts.
- PFAS-FLUX contributes an open and auditable workflow for sparse PFAS site-investigation data.
- The hydrogeologic contribution is the explicit connection between control-plane load, receptor-load exceedance probability and data-priority metrics.
- The synthetic benchmark demonstrates reproducibility without proprietary field data.

Avoid claiming that the paper is the first PFAS mass-flux assessment. PFAS-specific mass-flux studies and mass-discharge reports already exist.
